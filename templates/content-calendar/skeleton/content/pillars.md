## Overview

Content pillars are the thematic foundations for all content production. Every piece of content should map to exactly one pillar. Pillars ensure consistency, prevent topic drift, and make it easy to balance coverage across themes.

---

## Pillar 1: Thought Leadership

### Purpose

Establish the team as credible, opinionated voices in the problem space. Build trust with the target audience before they enter a buying cycle.

### Target Audience

- Operations managers evaluating approaches
- Engineering leaders researching solutions

### Topics

- Industry trends and analysis
- Point-of-view pieces on common practices
- Data-driven insights and original research
- Predictions and forward-looking commentary

### Content Mix

| Format | Frequency | Channel |
|---|---|---|
| Blog post | 2x per month | Blog, LinkedIn, Newsletter |
| LinkedIn post | 1x per week | LinkedIn |
| Guest contribution | 1x per quarter | Industry publication |

### Voice and Tone

- Confident and informed, not salesy
- Back claims with data or specific observations
- Acknowledge complexity rather than oversimplifying

---

## Pillar 2: Product Education

### Purpose

Help existing and prospective users understand how the product works, what problems it solves, and how to get the most out of it.

### Target Audience

- Users evaluating the product
- New users in their first 30 days
- Power users seeking advanced workflows

### Topics

- Feature deep-dives and walkthroughs
- Architecture and technical approach explanations
- Best practices and workflow guides
- Integration tutorials

### Content Mix

| Format | Frequency | Channel |
|---|---|---|
| Technical article | 1x per month | Blog, Dev community |
| Tutorial / how-to | 2x per month | Blog, Documentation |
| Short-form video | 1x per month | YouTube, Social |

### Voice and Tone

- Clear, precise, technically accurate
- Show, don't tell -- use examples and screenshots
- Assume intelligence but not context

---

## Pillar 3: Customer Stories

### Purpose

Build social proof by showing real outcomes from real users. Translate abstract product capabilities into concrete business results.

### Target Audience

- Prospects in evaluation and decision stages
- Executive sponsors who need ROI justification
- Existing customers who want validation of their choice

### Topics

- Customer case studies with before/after metrics
- Workflow transformation stories
- Industry-specific use case showcases
- Community spotlights

### Content Mix

| Format | Frequency | Channel |
|---|---|---|
| Case study | 1x per month | Blog, Email, Sales collateral |
| Customer quote graphic | 2x per month | LinkedIn, Twitter |
| Video testimonial | 1x per quarter | YouTube, Landing page |

### Voice and Tone

- Let the customer's voice lead
- Focus on outcomes, not features
- Include specific numbers whenever possible

---

## Pillar 4: Community and Culture

### Purpose

Build affinity with the audience beyond the product. Show the human side of the team and contribute to the broader community.

### Target Audience

- Broader professional community in the space
- Potential hires and partners
- Existing customers and advocates

### Topics

- Team perspectives and behind-the-scenes
- Open-source contributions and community involvement
- Event recaps and conference takeaways
- Hiring and culture posts

### Content Mix

| Format | Frequency | Channel |
|---|---|---|
| Social post | 2x per month | LinkedIn, Twitter |
| Blog post | 1x per quarter | Blog |
| Newsletter section | 1x per month | Newsletter |

### Voice and Tone

- Authentic and approachable
- Avoid corporate jargon
- Show real people and real work

## Pillar Allocation

Target content mix by volume across pillars:

| Pillar | Share of Content | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Thought Leadership | 30% | Builds top-of-funnel awareness and trust |
| Product Education | 30% | Drives evaluation and adoption |
| Customer Stories | 25% | Converts prospects and retains customers |
| Community and Culture | 15% | Builds long-term brand affinity |
