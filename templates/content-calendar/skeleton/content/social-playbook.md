## Social Strategy Overview

This playbook defines how to execute social media as part of the content program. Social is a distribution and engagement channel, not a standalone strategy. Every social post should trace back to a content pillar and support a campaign or editorial calendar item.

---

## Platform Guidelines

### LinkedIn

**Purpose:** Professional thought leadership and product credibility

**Audience:** Decision-makers, practitioners, industry peers

**Post Types:**

| Type | Frequency | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Insight post (text-only) | 2x/week | Engagement and reach |
| Content share | 1x/week | Traffic to blog |
| Customer highlight | 2x/month | Social proof |
| Team/culture post | 1x/month | Brand affinity |

**Best Practices:**

- Lead with an insight or question, not a link
- Native text posts outperform link shares by 3-5x on average
- Put links in the first comment, not the post body
- Use 2-3 relevant hashtags maximum
- Tag people and companies mentioned (with permission)
- Respond to every comment within 4 hours during business hours

**Formatting:**

- Short paragraphs (1-2 sentences)
- Use line breaks for readability
- Hook in the first line -- it determines whether people click "see more"
- Keep posts under 1300 characters for optimal engagement

---

### Twitter/X

**Purpose:** Real-time commentary and community engagement

**Audience:** Technical community, industry commentators, developers

**Post Types:**

| Type | Frequency | Goal |
|---|---|---|
| Commentary/insight | 3x/week | Visibility and engagement |
| Thread | 1x/week | Deep-dive on a topic |
| Content share | 2x/week | Traffic |
| Community engagement | Daily | Relationship building |

**Best Practices:**

- Be opinionated and concise
- Quote-tweet with commentary rather than plain retweets
- Threads work well for technical explanations and data breakdowns
- Engage with relevant conversations proactively
- Use threads for anything over 280 characters rather than cramming

## Content Adaptation Framework

When adapting content for social, follow this process:

### From Blog Post

1. Extract the single most interesting insight or data point
2. Write it as a standalone statement (no "we just published" framing)
3. Add context in 1-2 supporting sentences
4. End with a question or call to engage
5. Link to full article in comments (LinkedIn) or reply (Twitter)

### From Case Study

1. Pull the most compelling metric or quote
2. Frame it as "before → after" transformation
3. Create a customer quote graphic with the key result
4. Tag the customer (with approval)

### From Technical Article

1. Identify the "aha moment" or key technical decision
2. Write it as a "Here's why we chose X over Y" or "How X works under the hood" framing
3. Use diagrams or code snippets as images for visual engagement
4. Cross-post to relevant developer communities

## Engagement Protocol

### Inbound Comments

| Type | Response Time | Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Question | < 4 hours | Helpful, direct answer with link if applicable |
| Praise | < 8 hours | Thank and amplify |
| Criticism | < 4 hours | Acknowledge, clarify, take offline if needed |
| Spam | < 24 hours | Hide/report |

### Proactive Engagement

- Monitor 3-5 industry hashtags and keywords daily
- Engage with posts from customers, partners, and industry voices
- Share and comment on relevant third-party content weekly
- Join conversations where the team has genuine expertise to contribute
