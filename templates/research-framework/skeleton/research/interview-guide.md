## Interview Protocol

### Warm-Up (5 minutes)

Build rapport and set expectations. These questions are easy to answer and get the participant talking.

1. "Tell me about your role and what a typical day looks like."
2. "How long have you been in this role?"
3. "What tools do you use most frequently in your day-to-day work?"

> **Facilitation note:** Listen for signals about workflow patterns, frustrations, and tool opinions. Do not steer toward the research topic yet.

---

### Context Setting (10 minutes)

Understand the participant's environment and current practices before diving into specific behaviors.

4. "Walk me through how you currently handle [relevant workflow]. Start from the trigger that tells you it's time to do it."
5. "Who else is involved in this process? What's their role?"
6. "How often does this come up? Is it predictable or ad hoc?"
7. "What does 'done' look like for this task? How do you know it's complete?"

> **Facilitation note:** Ask for specific recent examples rather than generalizations. "Tell me about the last time you did this" is more useful than "How do you usually do this?"

---

### Core Exploration (20 minutes)

Dig into pain points, workarounds, and unmet needs. This is the heart of the interview.

8. "What's the hardest part of this process? Why?"
9. "When was the last time something went wrong with [workflow]? What happened?"
10. "What would you change about this process if you could change anything?"
11. "Have you tried other approaches or tools to make this easier? What happened?"
12. "If this process suddenly took half the time, what would you do with the extra time?"
13. "How do you know when the output of this process is accurate? What gives you confidence?"

> **Facilitation note:** Follow the participant's energy. When they describe a frustration with emotion, probe deeper with "Tell me more about that" or "Why is that particularly painful?"

---

### Concept Reaction (10 minutes)

_Use this section only for evaluative or mixed research. Skip for purely generative studies._

14. "I'd like to show you a rough concept and get your reaction. This is not a finished product — there are no wrong answers."

    _[Show mockup or describe concept]_

15. "What's your first reaction?"
16. "What would you expect to happen when you [specific interaction]?"
17. "What's confusing or unclear?"
18. "Would this fit into your current workflow? What would need to change?"

> **Facilitation note:** Watch for body language and hesitation. If the participant says "it's nice," probe with "What specifically is nice about it?" or "What's missing?"

---

### Wrap-Up (5 minutes)

19. "Is there anything about [topic] that I should have asked but didn't?"
20. "If you could give one piece of advice to the team building a solution for this, what would it be?"
21. "Would you be open to a follow-up conversation if we have more questions?"

## After the Interview

### Debrief Template

Complete within 1 hour of the interview:

- **Top 3 insights:** What surprised you or confirmed a hypothesis?
- **Quotes to capture:** Verbatim quotes that illustrate key findings
- **Hypothesis updates:** Which hypotheses were supported, challenged, or unchanged?
- **Follow-up questions:** What would you ask next time?

### Tagging Taxonomy

Use these tags when coding interview notes for synthesis:

| Tag | Description |
|---|---|
| `pain-point` | Described frustration or difficulty |
| `workaround` | Current coping mechanism |
| `unmet-need` | Expressed desire for something that doesn't exist |
| `workflow` | Description of current process steps |
| `tool-opinion` | Opinion about a specific tool |
| `trigger` | What initiates the workflow |
| `validation` | How accuracy is checked |
| `emotion` | Strong emotional response (positive or negative) |
