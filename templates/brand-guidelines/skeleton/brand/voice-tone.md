# Voice and Tone

Guidelines for how __BRAND_NAME__ communicates in written content across all channels.

## Voice

Voice is the consistent personality behind all __BRAND_NAME__ communication. It doesn't change based on context — it's who we are.

### Voice attributes

| Attribute | What it means | What it doesn't mean |
|---|---|---|
| **Clear** | Direct, jargon-free, scannable | Dumbed down or oversimplified |
| **Confident** | Assured, knowledgeable, decisive | Arrogant, dismissive, or pushy |
| **Helpful** | Anticipates needs, guides next steps | Patronizing or hand-holding |
| **Human** | Conversational, warm, approachable | Slangy, overly casual, or unprofessional |

### Voice in practice

**Do:** "Your deployment is live. Here's what to check first."
**Don't:** "Congratulations! Your super awesome deployment has been successfully completed!"

**Do:** "Something went wrong. Try again, or contact support if it keeps happening."
**Don't:** "Oops! An unexpected error occurred. Error code: 0x4F2A."

## Tone

Tone shifts based on context while the voice stays constant. A success message and an error message share the same voice but different tones.

### Tone spectrum

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Onboarding | Encouraging, guiding | "Let's set up your workspace. This takes about two minutes." |
| Success states | Affirming, brief | "Changes saved." |
| Error states | Calm, solution-oriented | "That file couldn't be uploaded. Check the format and try again." |
| Empty states | Helpful, inviting | "No projects yet. Create one to get started." |
| Destructive actions | Serious, clear | "This will permanently delete your workspace and all its data." |
| Documentation | Instructive, precise | "Pass the configuration object to the init function before calling start." |
| Marketing | Aspirational, clear | "Ship faster without sacrificing quality." |

## Writing principles

### Lead with the action

Put the most important information or the primary action first. Users scan — front-load what matters.

- **Do:** "Save your changes before leaving this page."
- **Don't:** "Please note that if you navigate away from this page, any unsaved changes may be lost."

### Be specific

Replace vague language with concrete details. Users need to know what happened and what to do next.

- **Do:** "Your file is too large. Maximum size is 10 MB."
- **Don't:** "There was a problem with your upload."

### Use active voice

Active voice is shorter, clearer, and more direct.

- **Do:** "We sent a confirmation email to your inbox."
- **Don't:** "A confirmation email has been sent."

### Write for scanning

- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
- Use headings and subheadings liberally
- Use lists for three or more related items
- Bold key terms and actions
- Put the conclusion first, then explain

## Content patterns

### Button labels

- Use verbs: "Save", "Create project", "Send invite"
- Be specific: "Delete workspace" not "Delete"
- Skip "Click here" or "Submit" — describe what happens

### Error messages

- State what happened
- Explain why (if known and helpful)
- Suggest what to do next
- Never blame the user

### Placeholder text

- Describe what goes in the field: "Enter your email address"
- Don't use placeholder as the only label
- Keep examples realistic: "jane@example.com" not "email"

### Confirmation dialogs

- Restate the action: "Delete this project?"
- Describe consequences: "This removes all files and cannot be undone."
- Make buttons match the action: "Delete project" / "Keep project"
